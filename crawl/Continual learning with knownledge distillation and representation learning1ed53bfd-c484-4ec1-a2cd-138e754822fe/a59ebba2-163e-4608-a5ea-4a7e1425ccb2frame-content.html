THESIS SUMMARY
1. Thesis title: Continual Learning with Knowledge Distillation and Representa-
tion Learning
2. Thesis author: Tran Tung Lam - 2021B
3. Thesis advisor: Prof. Than Quang Khoat
4. Keywords: Continual learning, Memory-based methods, Gradient projection-
based methods, Gradient conflict, Flat minima
5. Summary
(a) Reason for writing: In recent years, Continual Learning has gained trac-
tion in Machine Learning community due to its practical goal: to mimic
the endless learning process of humans This can greatly benefit real-life
deployment of many models such as autonomous cars as the models can
learn a sequence of tasks continually. Memory-based methods, a strong
candidate for this problem, however, so far are still limited in comparison
with traditional training.
(b) Aims, Object and Range of the study: Therefore, this thesis aims to im-
prove performance of memory-based Continual Learning methods by fo-
cusing on two problems: overfitting to the small buffer, and potential con-
flict between learning of old and new tasks. The research scope is incre-
mental classification problem.
(c) Contr ibutions: The thesis covers two representative classes of memory-
based methods: raw data replaying and gradient constraining. To reduce
overfitting, the thesis proposes to apply models sharpness minimization
technique. However, analyses of the hidden gradient conflict caused by
this technique for the two classes of methods are then given, from which
two methods are proposed to tackle such conflict. The obtained results
show significant improvements of the proposed methods.
(d) Methods: The methods are first inspired by a technique from mitigating
overfitting of neural networks. Then the hidden conflict when naively ap-
plying this technique is revealed by investigating the sharpness gradients
of old and new tasks and the improper projection of such gradient to the
old tasks feature subspace. From this, the proposed methods apply two
gradient alignment strategies, one is inspired from multi-task learning and
one is a novel objective function, to resolve such conflict.

(e) Conclusion: The thesis proposes two solutions to improve two represent-
ative classes of memory-based methods by minimizing model sharpness
and gradient conflict. With analyses and experimental justification, the
thesis shows the effectiveness of the methods, and discusses possible fu-
ture research directions.
